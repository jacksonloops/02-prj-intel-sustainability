Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how innovation and responsibility have shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore establish Intel Corporation, laying the groundwork for decades of technological leadership.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel introduces the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the modern computing revolution.

1978

8086 Processor

The 8086 processor establishes the x86 architecture that continues to power personal computers and servers today.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, enabling multitasking, improved memory management, and a significant leap in performance that reshaped personal computing.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel reaches its highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations. This milestone prompts long-term investments in renewable energy, chemical abatement, and energy-efficient manufacturing practices.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE strategy—Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, and Enabling—setting ambitious 2030 goals focused on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction across the semiconductor industry.

2022

Net-Zero by 2040

Intel commits to achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across global operations by 2040.

2023

99% Renewable Electricity

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, dramatically reducing operational carbon emissions.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, bringing together suppliers, governments, and industry leaders to collaborate on the future of sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.
